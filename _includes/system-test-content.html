Content Blocks Component

Standard Content Block:

Introduction to Fair Division

Fair division is a fundamental problem in mathematics, economics, and computer science. It addresses how to allocate resources among multiple parties in a way that each party considers fair according to their own valuation.

The field combines elements of game theory, algorithm design, and social choice theory to create practical solutions for real-world allocation problems.

Theory Content Block:

Envy-Free Allocation

Definition: An allocation is envy-free if no party prefers another party's share to their own.

This is one of the most important fairness criteria in fair division theory, ensuring that each participant is satisfied with their allocation relative to others.

Algorithm Content Block:

Steinhaus Procedure

A three-party fair division algorithm that extends the classical divide-and-choose protocol to multiple participants while maintaining proportionality guarantees.

  • Guarantees each party receives at least 1/n of their valuation
  • Requires O(n¬≤) Robertson-Webb queries
  • Works for any number of participants
